Every year for over 50 years, every IIT has put forth their best athletes, to compete in what is now the largest college sporting fest in the country. Conducted in rotation between the older IIT’s, the only interruptions to this annual event have been at times of war.

The first inter IIT Sports Meet was held in 1961 at Bombay. It was subsequently held in Kharagpur and then Madras. Over 9 editions of the meet, from 1970 to 1980, Madras took home the championship every single time. IIT Madras has the distinction of having the highest number of General Championships (as they were referred to then), with a staggering 19. All IITians who share a passion for sport and the drive to glorify their institute, congregate every October and December, to show their mettle and temerity. 16 IIT’s will share the battle ground this year, with 2 new IIT’s participating on a provisional basis. As teams vie for gold in the 51st edition of the meet, the competition promises to be intense, and the arena is likely to heat up. As tensions soar, and adrenaline rushes, a mega event the likes of which has seldom been seen will draw to close, leaving only one winner. The Sports Meet is an embodiment of the IIT commitment to excel, the endeavor to triumph, the hard work, sweat, blood and tears of every single participant, and the hopes of thousands of their comrades. The championship is not just a bragging right, it is so much more. With faster times, higher lifts, and tougher competition year after year, the 51st inter IIT Sports Meet is poised to bring us some MadrasAction.
